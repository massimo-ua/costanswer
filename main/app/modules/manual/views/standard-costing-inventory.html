Product inventory

This element reflects the quantity of the finished goods available at your storage at the beginning of the accounting period – the first month of your project. This value does not have direct impact on the cost of the product.

It refers to company's current inventory assets valuation in the Cost of Goods Manufactured (COGM) schedule. Inventory assets are: WIP, raw materials and finished goods. The COGM schedule is a very helpful managerial tool. It reports and adjusts the total manufacturing costs for the accounting period (month, year) in the work-in-process inventory account to calculate the cost of goods manufactured.

The amount of the COGM is transferred to the finished goods inventory account during the accounting period and is used to calculate cost of goods sold on the Income Statement Report. When comparing the accounting of several Income Statement Reports over time, you can track changes of your gross profit volumes. This helps you set future goals and strategies for sales, inventory and overhead.

COSTANSWER is using Weighted Average Periodic Inventory accounting method to compute the value of inventory. It means that the recorded amount of inventory represents a value somewhere between the oldest and newest units purchased, manufactured or sold. In other words, the cost valuation of the finished goods in stock by the end of the month depends on the average monthly cost per product.

Data input

Please, enter the quantity of the finished products available at you storage or warehouse at the beginning of the first month of the project.

When you are done with value, please, press button “Save”.

You can introduce changes to saved element simply by clicking on it and, after the changes are done, press button “Update”.

Important note: please, keep in mind, that service is intangible, so it cannot be stored. That is why it is impossible to have its inventory value at the beginning of the accounting period.

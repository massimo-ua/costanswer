Variable manufacturing overhead

Manufacturing overhead can be broken into 2 types –fixed manufacturing overhead (FMOH) and variable manufacturing overhead (VMOH).

Fixed manufacturing overhead costs incur every accounting period regardless of product quantities you manufacture.

Variable manufacturing overhead strictly depends on the number of units produced and reflects some costs that can be entirely assigned to the total batch of product or service – for example the cost of batch transportation, batch packaging, batch shipment cost, batch lubricants, batch electricity or water cost, or, for example, some outsourced service or cost.

Data input

“Name of overhead” - is a text input field. There is a certain restriction on the length of the name. Please, try to be to be laconic in your statements.

In both cases it means that your product can be evaluated as a standard quantity of units (per batch or per month) at entering-the-data stage corresponding to the standard currency amount of assigned VMOH.

As you can see on the screenshots the standard currency amount mathematically is the same per each month of the annual budget of VMOH however the example was based upon two different approaches. It’s just a matter of your convenience and your costing methodology.

The currency amount of variable manufacturing overhead has a crucial direct impact on the product cost and is very important for project calculations.

All values must be free of taxes as sales tax or VAT.

When you are done with text and value, please, press button “Save”.

You can introduce changes to saved element simply by clicking on it and, after changes are done, press button “Update”.

Important note: please, keep in mind, that variable manufacturing overhead costs strictly depend on the level of production you are generating.

Variable Manufacturing Overhead

Manufacturing overhead can be broken into 2 types – fixed manufacturing overhead (FMOH) and variable manufacturing overhead (VMOH).

Fixed manufacturing overhead costs incur every accounting period regardless of product quantities you manufacture.

Variable manufacturing overhead strictly depends on the number of units produced and reflects some costs that can be entirely assigned to the total batch of product or service. Examples of these include things directly assigned to the batch are batch transportation, packaging, shipment cost, lubricants, may be some gas, electricity or water cost, or some outsourced service or cost.

Data entry fields:

“Name of variable overhead” is a text field. There is a restriction on the length of the field; please be brief.

“Standard cost” - depending on the standard quantity approach you chose in “Product/Service settings”, this field corresponds to the standard currency amount of VMOH required either for the “Standard quantity per batch” approach.

As you can see on the screenshots the standard currency amount mathematically is the same for each month of the annual budget of VMOH; however, the example was based upon two different approaches. It’s just a matter of choice and your cost methodology.

The currency amount of variable manufacturing overhead has a crucial direct impact on the product cost and is very important for project calculations.

All values must be free of taxes such as sales tax or VAT.

When you are done entering this amount, press the “Save” button.

You can change a saved item by clicking on it. After the changes are done, press the “Update” button.

Important note: Keep in mind that the variable manufacturing overhead cost strictly depends on the level of production you are generating.

Fixed Manufacturing Overhead Settings

COSTANSWER uses 2 approaches to apply fixed manufacturing overhead (FMOH) – “full cost applied” and “predetermined overhead rate”. What is the difference? We have divided data entry of FMOH into 8 groups – indirect labor, indirect materials, rent, depreciation and others. If you know the exact values of these 8 groups in the coming months, you can enter them using “full cost applied” button.

Screen 1

If you do not know the exact cost of FMOH, you can use “predetermined overhead rates” button to calculate the approximate amount of indirect cost for each product or service in your product mix.

Screen 2

What is your allocation base? It really depends upon your type of business. If your business requires a lot of manual labor and you hire many worker, probably you can probably choose “Labor hours” or “Direct labor” option. If your company has a lot of machinery used during the manufacturing process, perhaps you can use “Machine hours” option. If your production consumes a lot of raw materials you might choose “Direct materials” option. Also, you can ask your accountant what allocation base is used in your ERP or accounting program (QuickBooks, Sage, SAP and others).

FMOH settings require both manual input of percentage rate and choosing the value you need from the drop-down list of options.

If you cannot decide the allocation base, you can choose the “Prime cost” option. Prime cost is the cost of direct materials used and direct labor applied during the manufacturing process. The cost of 2 key elements of the product cost allows for the distribution of overhead more evenly.

Important note: Keep in mind, that fixed manufacturing overhead costs occur no matter what level of production or sales you are generating. That is why these expenses must be paid out of the company's cash, regardless of volume of sales.

Indirect materials

Indirect materials are part of FMOH and are the materials that are used in a production process, but are not directly traceable to a cost object. Sometimes these materials are not monetarily significant or not easily trackable. For example, many companies use different types of lubricants for their machinery or restaurants use different spices while preparing different sauces or dishes.

Indirect indirect materials often have significant costs the business. Consider the cost of different tools, measurement instruments and devices, transportation vehicles, loading equipment, cleaning supplies, etc.

Data Entry Fields:

COSTANSWER helps you to track each kind of these indirect materials.

The cost amount you are putting in must reflect the average expected monthly cost of indirect material. Cost values must be free of taxes such as sales tax or VAT.

When you are done entering the name and amount, press the “Add” button.

You can change a saved item by clicking on it. After changes are done, press the “Update” button to be redirected to the empty form for a new item.

Production managers salaries

Expenses for production managers salaries are part of FMOH. Production managers are responsible for overseeing the operations of a given manufacturing facility, scheduling employees and managing the workload and projects. Usually their work does not relate to the production of the specific product or service. That is why the salary expenses of this personnel incur no matter what level of production or sales you are generating.

Data Entry Fields:

COSTANSWER helps you to track salary expenses of each production manager. The form includes name\title, average monthly salary value, the amount of payroll taxes covered by the employer and the annual growth rate percentage.

When you are done entering the name and amount, press the “Add” button.

You can change a saved item by clicking on it. After changes are done, press the “Update” button to be redirected to the empty form for a new item.

Production facilities insurance

Expenses for production facility's insurance are part of FMOH. In the event of a fire, property damage, vandalism, or theft, it is important for business owners to have adequate insurance for the production facilities (buildings, warehouses, storages and other premises) in place to cover possible damage.

Indirect labor

Expenses for indirect labor are part of FMOH and include salaries of maintenance personnel, supervisors, drivers, quality inspectors and many other employees that do not have a direct connection to the production of the specific product. Keep in mind, that sales or administrative (office) personnel are not part of indirect labor.

Production machinery rent

Expenses for renting (or leasing) production machinery are part of FMOH. Renting allows business owners to have full use of an asset, such as machinery, vehicles or equipment over a fixed period without having the responsibility of owning it. Renting typically involves a short time period, often 1 year maximum, with the option to extend after the term at the discretion of both parties. Rentals are more suited to the temporary use of assets when the expectation is that it will not be needed long-term.

Production facilities depreciation

Expenses for production facilities depreciation are part of FMOH. Depreciation is the loss in value of a property over time due to physical deterioration. This loss can be calculated by accountants using depreciating schedule and can be included in the cost of the product when a manufacturer's products are sold. When the manufactured products are held in inventory, some of the depreciation associated with the manufacturing process will be included in the cost of the inventory as well.

To find out the monthly amount of production facilities depreciation you can consult your company accountant.

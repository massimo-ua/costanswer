Direct materials

Direct materials are the raw materials that become part of the product. For example flour, eggs, water and salt are the raw materials to make bread.

Data input

“Name of raw material” is a text input field. There is a certain restriction on the length of the name. Please, try to be to be laconic in your statements. For the moment we do not provide raw material input forms with any additional technical information because do not see the necessity for the users to fill it in.

“Measurement unit” is a text input field. Every raw material must be measured in certain units of length or weight.

The amount of the raw materials used during product’s production has a crucial direct impact on the product cost and is very important for project calculations.

“Price per unit” input field corresponds to the current average purchasing price during the first month of the project. In other words you are setting the 100% price level at the start of the project.

The purchasing cost of the raw material used during product’s production has a crucial direct impact on the product cost and is very important for project calculations.

Price values must be free of taxes as sales tax or VAT.

“Season price change” input form helps you to model price changes expressed in percent – rise or decline - during the year. In case you forecast the drop of the purchasing price you must enter the value with negative sign “minus”.

For example, April is the first month of your project. Its purchasing price is 100%. If you project 2% price growth in May, you enter the input field with “2”. That will mean that price will grow 102% compared to April’s 100% price level.

If you think that in June price level is going to drop 2% down, you enter the input field with “-2” value. That will mean that price will drop down to 98% compared to April’s 100% price level.

“Normal waste” is a loss of quantities of raw material due to any reason either because of its nature of the material itself, the nature of the process or some other conditions. For example, many materials contain water that tends to vapor. That leads to reduction of material weight and quantity during the transportation, warehousing or manufacturing process.

“Cost of raw material beginning” is the value of the particular raw material at the warehouse determined during inventory or according to your accounting reports and expressed in average purchasing prices of the previous periods. It does not have direct impact on the product cost.

It refers to company's current inventory assets valuation and its value should be included in the Cost of Goods Manufactured (COGM) Schedule. This schedule is a very helpful managerial tool. It reports and adjusts the total manufacturing costs for the accounting period (month, year) in the work-in-process inventory account to calculate the cost of goods manufactured.

The amount of COGM is transferred to the finished goods inventory account during the accounting period and is used to calculate cost of goods sold on the Income Statement Report. When comparing the accounting of several Income Statement Reports over time, you can track changes of your gross profit volumes. This helps you set future goals and strategies for sales, inventory and overhead.

COSTANSWER is using Weighted Average Periodic Inventory accounting method to compute the value of inventory. It means that the recorded amount of inventory represents a value somewhere between the oldest and newest units purchased, manufactured or sold. In other words, the cost valuation of the raw material in stock by the end of the month depends on its average monthly purchasing cost.

In the nearest future we plan to create some tools to allow users to use all or some quantities of raw materials available at the beginning of the project to reduce the budgeted volume of material needed to be purchased during the first month of the project.

If you have doubts and hesitations regarding this value the best case scenario is to avoid entering any values at this stage. Simply put “0” and press “Save” button.

If you start manufacturing of the new product you, probably, will not have any values of the cost of raw material beginning in your projections because there was no business activity in the past.

All values must be free of taxes as sales tax or VAT.

“Safety stock” is a nice tool to keep your projected inventory investments on the economically reasonable level. Using instant calculation option you can model the best case scenario for raw material purchases.

This parameter does not have direct impact on the cost of the product but has a direct relation to the financial results of your project via section “Direct materials purchased” of the project’s Cost of Goods Manufactured Schedule. That is why, please, avoid unnecessary stock purchases during your planning because that can impact your project’s financial results.

When you are done with text and values, please, press button “Save”.

You can introduce changes to saved element simply by clicking on it and, after the changes are done, press button “Update”.

Important note: please, be very careful while choosing raw material’s measurement unit. In case you have big quantity of different materials to be used during the production of one product you must follow the common denominator of weights or sizes. If you start measuring first material in liters it makes no sense to skip to tons measuring the second material.

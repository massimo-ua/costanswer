Variable vs fixed

The behavior of costs can be Variable or Fixed. Variable costs fluctuate depending on the volume of sales or production. That includes direct materials and utilities, direct labor salaries and any other direct and indirect costs that will change depending on actual production or sales volume.

Fixed costs occur no matter what level of production or sales you are generating. Costs like rent, insurance, lease payments and office administration salaries are all examples of fixed costs.

Why is it important for any type of business to trace the volumes of variable and fixed costs carefully? The answer is obvious: high fixed expenses must be paid out of the company's cash regardless of the volume of sales. That is why you have to review all your operations carefully, to identify and avoid excessive fixed expenses.

“Per Month” or “Per Batch” approach

COSTASWER allows you to enter raw-materials and labor-hour values using either “Per Month” or “Per Batch” approach.

For example, you know that you need 800 square feet of denim to manufacture a “batch” of 45 jeans. And it takes 2 days to make that quantity.

Or you can know that in order to manufacture 1400 pairs of jeans per month you need 2600 square feet of denim.

In both cases it means that your product can be evaluated as a standard quantity of units (per batch or per month) at entering-the-data stage corresponding to the standard volume of purchased and assigned raw materials, applied labor hours and manufacturing overhead.

We admit that in some cases it’s more convenient to “assemble” the cost of the product using “per unit” approach. You can easily achieve that if you simply consider 1 unit as a batch of units at entering-the-data stage.

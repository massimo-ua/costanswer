Fixed manufacturing overhead allocation

Allocation (distribution) of company fixed manufacturing overhead to products and services is really tricky and challenging. Fixed manufacturing overhead relates to a production as a whole. As a result, overhead costs can be absorbed (allocated, apportioned, distributed) by a specific product or a job depending on different cost driving bases (allocation bases).

COSTANSWER uses 5 types of fixed manufacturing overhead (MOH) allocation bases – direct labor, direct materials, number of working hours, prime cost and number of machine hours.

Also COSTANSWER uses 2 approaches to apply manufacturing overhead – “full cost applied” and “predetermined overhead rate”. What is the difference?

We have divided data entry of fixed manufacturing overhead into 8 groups – indirect labor, indirect materials, rent, amortization and others. If you know the exact values of these 8 groups in coming months, you can enter them using “full cost applied” button.

If you do not know the exact cost of manufacturing overheads, you can use “predetermined overhead rates” button to calculate the approximate (!) number of indirect costs per each product or service in your product mix.

For example, you might estimate that in upcoming fiscal year the total cost of all 8 groups will be $1 000 000 and the annual amount of direct labor will cost $800,000. As a result, you can set the predetermined overhead rate using allocation base of “number of working hours” at 1000000/800000*100% = 125%. It means that for every dollar of direct labor, you will assign $1,25 (125%) of manufacturing overhead to a product or service.

The use of a predetermined overhead rate enables the company to quickly determine the approximate total cost of each product or service it produces or delivers.

Of course there will always be a difference between the actual overhead cost incurred during the period and the amount of overhead applied using predetermined overhead rates. COSTANSWER variance analysis tracks such differences and shows whether actual fixed MOH was over-applied or under-applied per project and per product or service.

Using the logic of forecasting vs actual result, COSTANSWER allows to use predetermined overhead rate only in FORECAST time mode for all 3 costing methods.

It is not hard to define and assign fixed manufacturing overhead to a product or a service that is using predetermined overhead rates. But how to find the proper amount of fixed manufacturing overhead that should be assigned to each product or service using “full cost applied” button if your product mix consists of 30, 50, 100, or even of 1000 items?

There are several mathematical approaches to solve this problem and these algorithms are used by all ERPs and accounting systems available on the market.

Imagine you are producing 3 different products. Your monthly costs incurred:

Product #1: direct materials cost incurred is $324 per batch, direct labor per batch - $234, batch quantity manufactured – 19 units.

Product #2: direct materials cost incurred is $240 per batch, direct labor per batch - $210, batch quantity manufactured – 23 units.

Product #3: direct materials cost incurred is $167 per batch, direct labor per batch - $256, batch quantity manufactured – 27 units.

Your monthly recorded and “fully applied” fixed manufacturing overhead cost is $1500.

Question: how would you allocate fixed MOH among 3 different products? What logical approach would you choose to find a proper “portion” of fixed MOH for each product?

COSTANSWER uses a so-called “multiple allocation” method to distribute an appropriate portion of indirect costs to every product or service. This method consists of several steps of calculations. Using our example we:

Step 1. Select activities (driver, allocation base) to absorb fixed manufacturing overhead. We choose allocation base called “prime cost”. Prime cost is the sum of direct materials and labor.

Step 2. Find the total value of prime cost for all products: $324 + $234 + $240 + $210 + $167 + $256 = $1431

Step 3. Find the allocation ratio % for each product.

Product 1 – (($324 + $234) / $1431) x 100% = 38,99%

Product 2 – (($240 + $210) / $1431) x 100% = 31,45%

Product 3 – (($167+$256) / $1431) x 100% = 29,56%

Step 4. Using allocation ratio we calculate the portion of manufacturing overhead per each product batch.

Product 1 – ($1500/100%) x 38,99% = $584,90

Product 2 – ($1500/100%) x 31,45% = $471,70

Product 3 – ($1500/100%) x 29,56% = $443,40

Step 5. Now, let’s find out the manufacturing cost per unit:

Product 1 – ($324 + $234 + $584,90) / 19 = $60,15

Product 2 – ($240 + $210 +$471,70) / 23 = $40,07

Product 3 – ($167 + $256 + $443,40) / 27 = $32,08

The complexity of this approach allows to allocate fixed manufacturing overhead in a more accurate way. COSTASWER allows to allocate fixed manufacturing overhead to hundreds of products you produce after you choose allocation base.

Our “multiple allocation” method works automatically in both forecast and actual time modes after you press “full cost applied” button.

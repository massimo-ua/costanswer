COSTANSWER provides each of your projects with serious cost analysis used by finance professionals.

What can these ratios and values tell you about your product or service?

Indicator Explanation
Product revenue Calculated by multiplying the unit price by the number of units sold
Sales mix ratio Shows relative percentage of each product your company sells
Net Target Income unit Difference between unit selling price and cost of goods manufactured
Net Target Income batch Difference between batch selling price and cost of goods manufactured
Contribution margin unit Measures product's gross operating margin per unit in local currency
Contribution margin batch Measures product's gross operating margin per batch in currency units
Contribution margin ratio Measures product batch gross operating margin as a percentage
Return on sales batch Measures net income earned for each dollar of sales
Variable cost unit Direct materials and direct labor costs per unit
Variable cost batch Direct materials and direct labor costs per batch
Fixed cost unit Fixed manufacturing overheads per unit
Fixed cost batch Fixed manufacturing overheads per batch
Break-Even point units The volume of sales for which a business makes no profit. Expressed in units
Break-Even point $ The volume of sales for which a business makes no profit. Expressed in currency units
Margin of safety batch $ The reduction in sales that can occur before the break-even point is reached. Expressed in currency units
Margin of safety batch % The reduction in sales that can occur before the breakeven point is reached. Expressed as a percentage
DOL Degree of operating leverage measures how revenue growth translates into growth of operating income
